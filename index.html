Indian Handicrafts: Products & History

1. Madhubani Paintings

Description: Madhubani paintings are a traditional art form from the Mithila region of Bihar, India. These intricate paintings are characterized by their vibrant colors and elaborate patterns depicting mythological themes, nature, and daily life.

Materials: Handmade paper, natural colors

Origin: Bihar, India

History: Originating from the Mithila region, this art form dates back to ancient times. Traditionally, Madhubani paintings were created by women of the region on the walls of their homes during festivals and important events. Over time, the art form has gained international recognition and is now celebrated for its unique aesthetic and cultural significance.

Price: $50 - $150 (varies by size and detail)

Shipping: Free shipping within India. International shipping costs vary.

2. Kanjivaram Sarees

Description: Kanjivaram sarees are exquisite silk sarees known for their rich colors, intricate designs, and luxurious texture. They are often worn during weddings and ceremonial occasions in South India.

Materials: Pure silk

Origin: Kanchipuram, Tamil Nadu, India

History: The art of weaving Kanjivaram sarees began in the 5th century in the town of Kanchipuram. These sarees are renowned for their durability and elegance. Traditional Kanjivaram weaving involves intricate patterns and borders that are woven with gold or silver threads, making each saree a masterpiece of craftsmanship.

Price: $200 - $500 (depends on design and silk quality)

Shipping: Free shipping within India. International shipping costs are additional and vary based on destination.

3. Rajasthani Blue Pottery

Description: Rajasthani blue pottery is known for its vibrant blue and turquoise glazes. It features intricate patterns and designs and is used for making decorative items and tableware.

Materials: Clay, natural dyes

Origin: Jaipur, Rajasthan, India

History: This craft dates back to the Mughal period when artisans from Persia brought the technique to India. The blue pottery of Rajasthan is distinguished by its unique glaze, which is achieved by using a special mixture of quartz, and the art form has evolved to include a variety of designs and applications over the centuries.

Price: $30 - $80 (varies by item size and complexity)

Shipping: Free shipping within India. International shipping costs vary based on location.

4. Pashmina Shawls

Description: Pashmina shawls are renowned for their softness and warmth. Made from the fine wool of the Pashmina goat, these shawls are highly valued for their luxurious feel and intricate hand-embroidered designs.

Materials: Pashmina wool

Origin: Kashmir, India

History: The tradition of weaving Pashmina shawls dates back to ancient Kashmir, where artisans have been crafting these shawls for centuries. The fine quality of Pashmina wool, combined with intricate embroidery techniques, makes these shawls a symbol of elegance and luxury.

Price: $150 - $400 (depends on wool quality and embroidery)

Shipping: Free shipping within India. International shipping costs vary based on destination.

5. Brass Diya Lamps

Description: Brass diya lamps are traditional oil lamps used in Hindu rituals and festivals. They are characterized by their intricate designs and are often used to illuminate homes during religious ceremonies.

Materials: Brass

Origin: India

History: The use of brass diyas dates back to ancient times when they were used to light temples and homes. The tradition of crafting these lamps involves detailed metalwork, and they hold a significant place in Indian culture and religious practices, symbolizing purity and enlightenment.

Price: $20 - $60 (varies by size and craftsmanship)

Shipping: Free shipping within India. International shipping costs vary based on location.
